The Sound of Competition

People often say that no product is truly original. Through comparative analysis, I explored competing companies looking to address the same issue through similar features to craft an efficient, user-friendly interface for my aggregator tool.
Role: Designer, Researcher
Project: Personal Project
Timeline: March 2024
RIVALRIES
To find the uniqueness of competing companies, I began researching organizations that utilized similar aggregator functions: Spotify, Amazon, and Gmail. I considered how these companies organize their features to add to a favorites list or shopping cart, remove from the list, or filter and sort through the list. After exploration and research, I captured my discoveries in a competitive analysis diagram, focusing on the different features implemented by the competing companies.
ANALYSIS RESULTS
The competitive analysis shares that two websites did not include an icon indicating that an item had been added to the aggregated list. The lack of identification confuses the user about whether the item already exists in the list. Only two applications allow a user to filter through the aggregated list; filtering increases a user’s effectiveness and organization of their aggregated list. Additionally, Gmail and Amazon apply conceptual models to demonstrate physical objects that users would rely on to perform virtual actions in real life, such as the envelope icon and the shopping cart.
INTEGRATING MY RESEARCH
I consolidated my findings to brainstorm my own unique interface, focusing on favoriting songs, filtering based on the rating or the genre, and sorting based on a song's length. I learned I wanted to indicate when an item had been added or removed from the favorites list to limit user confusion. I also wanted to see the contents of the aggregated list while being able to add and remove from it simultaneously. Lastly, I considered the importance of visual awareness surrounding the features that could be filtered or sorted to allow for efficient user practice.
THE FINAL PRODUCT
I used my competitive analysis and conceptual ideas to create a clean, organized interface that demonstrates filtering, sorting, and aggregating abilities. I designed a dashboard holding a list of song cards containing their cover photo, artist, title, length, rating (out of 5 stars), and genre. I have two filterable categories enabling users to filter songs based on their ratings and genres. I created a sorting functionality that organizes the song cards based on the respective song’s length. Each song can be favorited and added to the aggregator list. A user can see how many songs are in the favorites list, the songs’ titles and lengths, and can remove the songs from this list. The song card itself also displays if the item is in the aggregated list via the heart icon; the user can click this button on a song’s card to add or remove the song from the favorites list.
FEATURES
Adding and removing from the favorites list
Filtering based on genre and rating
Sorting by song length
TAKEAWAYS
Through the research process, I enhanced my abilities to evaluate competitive companies and create a comparative analysis. I explored the benefits and disadvantages of competing companies that implement similar solutions. I utilized my findings to brainstorm a user-friendly design and interface that allows users to add, remove, filter, and sort from an aggregated list.
